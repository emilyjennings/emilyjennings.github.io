About Emily

I love tech, startups, and innovation. These are the things I surround myself with lately.

Learning is everything to me, especially when it comes to new tech skills, and have been taking courses in front-end web development, UX and UI.

Having studied philosophy extensively, I found my way into teaching in 2009. After teaching for a year in North Carolina, I lived and worked in South Korea for five years, and loved it. Before that, I lived in New Zealand for five years! I can never get enough of what human beings have to share with each other.

Empathy and communication really come into play when I am meeting people and helping them solve big problems. I love people. I look for deep, authentic connections and view people as whole human beings. I enjoy listening to the problems that others are facing and coming up with creative and caring solutions.

I have big dreams. I want to do everything in my power to help women become leaders in the tech space, and to help everyone learn skills to change the world through tech. I also want to become a "wizard programmer".

I am currently living in Philadelphia, PA and I love it.

Questions

What is the craziest thing you've ever done?

Moving to South Korea was easy, but the craziest thing was after living there for five years I decided to leave! I had a husband and baby at the time. We sold almost all of our belongings in just a couple months. It was amazing allowing myself to let go of so many material things that I thought I "needed" and moving on!

What is the most creative thing you've ever done?

Creative endeavors are addictive for me; beyond coding, I sew many of my own clothes and I have written short stories. The most creative and successful thing I've ever done was write a short story that won an honorable mention in a prestigous short story contest. It was called The Hole in the Basement Closet and it was a surrealistic story about two cats trapped in a house who had strange relationships with a hole in the basement closet. All metaphors!

Who do you admire most?

She may read this, and I assume she already knows - but the person I admire most since I've moved to Philly is Felicite Moorman, CEO of StratIS and Bulogics. I want to study every decision she makes as a leader. She is an incredibly inspirational person when it comes to work culture, the tech industry, and sustainability.
