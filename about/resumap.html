Let's start at the beginning. (This page is under construction.)

I'm from College Park, Maryland.

In high school, I thought living in the suburbs of DC was devastatingly boring and I wanted to see the world. So, I left! (Spolier alert: I come back later.)

Rio, Kyiv, Pretoria

Just to mention these, as a teenager I went on some exchange programs through scholarships and saving up money I earned working in retail.

University of Otago

After high school, I had to be far away for some reason, so I went as far as possible and studied philosophy at Otago in Dunedin, New Zealand. I had so much fun that I stayed and got a Master's degree. Little did I know then, that would be a big influence on me when I got into coding.

D.C. 2007-2010

I worked in various admin-type jobs in DC for a bit until I realized I wanted to do something with my career that made me feel like I was actually impacting the world. So, I became a teacher!

Fayetteville, NC

After a year living in this town in N.C., the home to one of the largest military bases in the world, I was ready to take my teaching skills abroad and live another part of my life overseas for what I thought would be only a year.

Jeju Island

It ended up being 5 years. I loved teaching Korean public school kids so much, I just stayed and kept renewing my contract until I was married and had a baby, and then it was time to come home.

Philadelphia

It took a long time to transition to a new career trajectory after Korea. I finally landed in tech, where I felt I could make the most impact through innovation and community. I worked at a coding bootcamp. I started coding. I fell in love.

Also, Philly is literally the best.

D.C. Again

To be even closer to family, I finally made it back home to D.C. where I currently reside. I live in Adams Morgan where things are pretty great. I code every day. I try my best to accidentally be a hipster.
